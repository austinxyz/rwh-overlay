## Moat

**Narrow / thesis-stage / regulatory-dependent.**

### What OKLO has
- **Advanced reactor design IP**: Aurora fast-spectrum metal-fueled reactor —
  smaller footprint, passive safety, recycles spent fuel
- **DOE site authorization at INL**: First-mover advantage at Idaho National
  Laboratory pilot site; uses DOE authorization path (not standard NRC) for
  pilot, enabling faster deployment
- **$2.4B cash runway**: Largest balance sheet among non-utility SMR names;
  covers ~4–5 years of current burn + capex ramp
- **Hyperscaler ecosystem**: Meta 1.2 GW Ohio prepayment + Switch 12 GW LOI +
  Equinix $25M prepay = Tier-1 datacenter buyer relationships validated
- **Sam Altman brand halo**: Board chair, 2.6% stake; provides AI-sector
  sourcing and credibility with hyperscalers
- **Kiewit Nuclear construction partner**: Lead constructor selected July
  2025; experienced nuclear EPC

### What OKLO doesn't have
- **Zero operating reactor**: First commercial unit target 2027 late / 2028 early
- **No NRC Combined License issued**: COLA Phase 1 submitted 2025;
  multi-year review
- **No firm revenue contracts**: All 14–18 GW pipeline is **non-binding LOI**
  or framework agreement
- **Competitors with real milestones**: NuScale has NRC design approval;
  Kairos Power has Google 500 MW binding deal; TerraPower has Bill Gates
  + DOE funding
- **Execution track record**: Nuclear projects historically overshoot
  budget 50–200% and timeline 2–5 years
- **Nuclear-specific risks**: fuel supply (HALEU), waste disposal
  legal/political, public-opposition siting delays

### Verdict
**Narrow, thesis-stage moat with significant competitive contest.** The story
is real (AI datacenter power demand + nuclear renaissance + SMR technical
maturation) but OKLO's specific first-mover claim is contested. This is
a bet on *execution quality and pace* vs. Kairos / NuScale / TerraPower —
not a structural-moat story.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **"Will Aurora-INL achieve commercial operation by late 2027 — and will
> one or more 12-GW-scale LOIs (Switch, Meta) convert to a binding PPA
> before 2027 — validating OKLO as the first profitable SMR platform
> before Kairos/NuScale/TerraPower claim hyperscaler relationships?"**

All else is corollary. Timeline slippage by even 12 months on Aurora-INL
triggers major thesis damage because:
1. Nuclear projects that miss initial COD typically slip 2–5 years
2. Competitors (Kairos + Google) are on parallel timelines with stronger
   commercial commitment
3. OKLO's $12.6B valuation prices substantial pipeline conversion at
   premium PPA rates — any delay pushes this cash flow beyond rational
   discount horizon
