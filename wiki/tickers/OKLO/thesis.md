## 1. Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Raison d'être**: AI hyperscale datacenters need baseload carbon-free power
at gigawatt scale within 3–5 years. The grid cannot deliver it. Solar/wind
can't provide 24×7 baseload. Natural gas triggers emissions commitments
breaches. Existing nuclear fleet is fully contracted. **Small Modular
Reactors (SMRs) are the only technology-ready answer** — but must get
through regulatory, construction, and fuel-supply hurdles.

Oklo's **Aurora Powerhouse** is a 15–75 MW fast-spectrum liquid-metal-cooled
reactor using HALEU (high-assay low-enriched uranium) fuel. Design
features: passive safety, modular factory production, recyclable spent
fuel, 20-year refueling cycle. Target customers: hyperscale datacenters,
military/industrial sites, remote utility grids.

**Strategic position as of 2026-04-23**:
- Groundbreaking at Idaho National Laboratory (INL) for Aurora-INL
  pilot (Sept 2025)
- Combined License Application (COLA) Phase 1 submitted; PDC topical report
  accepted by NRC on accelerated timeline
- Meta 1.2 GW prepayment agreement (Ohio campus)
- Switch 12 GW master power agreement (20-year, non-binding)
- Equinix $25M prepayment for 500 MW
- Diamondback Energy 50 MW LOI
- **Total pipeline: 14–18 GW (all non-binding)**
- Commercial operation target: **late 2027 / early 2028**

**Pivotal Question**: Will Aurora-INL actually achieve commercial operation
by late 2027, and will Meta and/or Switch convert their agreements into
binding PPAs in time to validate OKLO's $12.6B valuation before competing
SMR platforms (Kairos, NuScale, TerraPower) claim hyperscaler share?

## 5. Competitive Moat

### SMR Competitive Landscape

| Company | Design | Key Milestone | Hyperscaler Deal |
|---------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **OKLO** | Aurora (fast-spectrum, metal-cooled) | COLA submitted; ground broken INL | Meta 1.2 GW prepay |
| **Kairos Power** | KP-FHR (molten-salt-cooled) | NRC construction permit 2023 | **Google 500 MW binding** |
| **NuScale** | VOYGR (LWR-based, 77 MW) | NRC design certification 2023 | Lost UAMPS deal 2023 |
| **TerraPower** | Natrium (sodium-cooled, 345 MW) | Wyoming site broken ground 2024 | DOE ARDP funding, Gates |
| **X-energy** | Xe-100 (HTGR, 80 MW) | DOE partnership | Dow Chemical, Amazon AWS |
| **Holtec** | SMR-300 (LWR-based) | Palisades restart plan | — |
| **GE-Hitachi BWRX-300** | LWR-based, 300 MW | Ontario construction 2025 | Hyperscaler interest |

### OKLO's specific moat
- **Smaller capacity (15–75 MW)** — matches hyperscaler datacenter scale
  (individual facilities 50–500 MW); competitors mostly larger blocks
- **Metal fuel + recycling** — potential long-term fuel cost advantage
- **DOE site authorization** — only SMR pursuing pilot via DOE path (faster
  than NRC COLA)
- **Altman brand** — OpenAI + YC networks vs. Gates (TerraPower),
  Google (Kairos), Amazon (X-energy)

### OKLO's competitive disadvantages
- **Kairos has binding Google deal**; OKLO has only prepayments (Meta,
  Equinix)
- **NuScale has NRC approval**; OKLO does not yet
- **TerraPower has $2B DOE funding**; OKLO self-funded via equity markets
- **First-of-kind reactor class** (fast-spectrum, metal-cooled) = higher
  technical and licensing risk
- **No fuel supply at scale yet** — HALEU is supply-constrained globally

### Verdict
**Moat is thesis-stage and contested.** The AI-datacenter-nuclear thesis is
sound but OKLO's leadership within SMR space is not secured. Any one of
Kairos, NuScale, or X-energy could emerge as hyperscaler preferred
platform. Current valuation implies OKLO becomes a dominant winner — a
plausible but not probable outcome.
