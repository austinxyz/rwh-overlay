and **SiC** (silicon carbide) — pivoting out of low-margin mobile chargers
into high-power AI datacenter, grid, EV, and industrial applications.

## Moat

**Narrow and contested.** Navitas has genuine IP in GaNFast™ (integrated GaN
power ICs with drive + protection on-die) and GeneSiC™ (from the 2022
acquisition). But:

- GaN competitors: Infineon, Power Integrations, STMicro, Transphorm, EPC
- SiC competitors: Wolfspeed, onsemi, STM, Infineon — all larger, deeper pockets
- AI datacenter win with NVIDIA is **non-exclusive** — NVIDIA can and will
  multi-source; pricing pressure inevitable
- Manufacturing is fab-lite (TSMC for GaN, GlobalFoundries partnership for US
  GaN starting later 2026) — good for capital efficiency, bad for margin
  defensibility in downcycles

Verdict: **Narrow moat, not yet commercially validated at scale** in the new
high-power segments.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **"Can Navitas translate a real-but-non-exclusive NVIDIA design-win and a
> US GaN manufacturing partnership into $250M+ in high-power revenue by
> 2028, and do so without another dilutive capital raise?"**

Everything else is corollary. At $4.26B mcap on $46M trailing revenue, the
market is already pricing in substantial success. The thesis-breaker is
execution pace on the pivot, not the thesis itself.
