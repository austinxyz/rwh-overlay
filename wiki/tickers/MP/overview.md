## Business in One Line

Owner/operator of **Mountain Pass** (Mojave Desert, CA) — the only integrated
rare-earth mining-and-processing complex in the Western Hemisphere — pivoting
from ore concentrate exporter to a fully integrated **"mine-to-magnet"**
domestic producer with DoD equity backing, a guaranteed NdPr price floor,
and downstream Apple/EV magnet offtakes.

## Moat

**Narrow-to-moderate and widening via policy moat.** Distinct from the other
overlay speculatives: MP has a *structural* moat source (irreplaceable asset +
government policy) rather than a pure execution moat.

### What MP has

- **Mountain Pass**: Only large-scale operational rare-earth mine in the
  Western Hemisphere. Contains one of the world's richest bastnaesite
  deposits. Produced **~50,000 MT REO concentrate in 2025** (~14% of global
  supply). Replicating this asset would take 10+ years and $2B+ of capex
  with no guarantee of equivalent ore grade.
- **Vertically integrated "mine-to-magnet" platform**: Stage 1 (mining) →
  Stage 2 (separation/processing at Mountain Pass) → Stage 3 (metal + alloy +
  sintered NdFeB magnets at Independence, Fort Worth TX). First U.S.
  end-to-end capability in decades.
- **DoD 10-year price floor of $110/kg on NdPr**: Eliminates commodity-price
  downside risk on the primary revenue product. If spot < $110/kg, Treasury
  pays the gap as a quarterly cash top-up. This is a *structural cash-flow
  guarantee*, not a loan.
- **DoD largest shareholder (15% as-converted)**: $400M preferred + warrants
  converting at $30.03 gives Pentagon aligned long-term interest. Political
  risk of rug-pull is asymmetrically low.
- **Apple $500M / 10-year magnet offtake**: $200M upfront + recycled-magnet
  supply for "hundreds of millions of devices" starting 2027. Validates
  pricing power on the downstream end of the value chain.
- **10X Facility + 100% DoD offtake**: The second magnet plant (Northlake,
  TX; $1.25B capex; commissioning 2028) has a *guaranteed* 100% offtake by
  DoD for 10 years post-commissioning. Revenue is contractually derisked,
  not just hoped for.
- **Geopolitical tailwind**: China's October 2025 export-control expansion
  (12 of 17 REEs restricted, extraterritorial reach, military-end-user ban)
  + 2025 tariff cycle make Western supply a **national-security mandate**,
  not a preference.

### What MP doesn't have

- **Scale vs. China**: Even after full ramp, MP's ~1,000 MT/year NdFeB
  magnet output is <1% of China's ~138,000 MT/year. MP is a strategic
  alternative, not a substitute.
- **Heavy rare earth capability (today)**: Mountain Pass is light-REE rich
  (Nd/Pr); heavy REEs (Dy/Tb) separation targeted for **mid-2026**.
  Competitors like Energy Fuels (UUUU) aiming at Dy/Tb commercial output
  in Q4 2026 — not yet decided.
- **Operational history at Stage 3**: Independence facility only produced
  first commercial magnets in 2025. Automotive-grade qualification cycle
  (typically 18–24 months) still to be completed.
- **Cost structure competitive with China**: Chinese rare-earth supply
  benefits from lax environmental standards, subsidies, and vertically
  captured demand. MP's unit economics rely on the DoD floor to be
  workable at current NdPr prices ($55–65/kg range historically).

### Verdict

**Narrow-to-moderate moat with widening policy reinforcement.** Stronger
structural moat than NVTS/POET/WOLF because the asset itself is irreplaceable
and the primary demand is politically mandated. But execution on Stage 3
magnet commercialization is still the binding constraint.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **"Will the 'mine-to-magnet' transition convert MP's strategic monopoly
> into compounding cash flow by 2028 — and does the DoD price floor + Apple
> offtake + 10X facility contractual offtake provide enough downside
> protection to justify paying ~48× FY2025 revenue at $10.7B mcap?"**

Everything else is corollary. Unlike NVTS (binary execution bet) or OKLO
(regulatory/construction timeline bet), MP's **Q1 2026 onwards should show
mechanical EBITDA expansion** as NdPr price floor takes effect, NdPr
production continues to double YoY, and magnet revenue begins mid-2026.

The thesis-breaker is slower-than-expected magnet-facility ramp or a
Democratic-administration reversal of the DoD partnership — both low
probability but not zero.
