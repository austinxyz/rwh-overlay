## Moat

**Narrow and unproven at scale, but architecturally distinctive.**

### What POET has
- **Optical Interposer™ IP portfolio**: Monolithic integration of lasers, modulators,
  detectors on a single substrate — vs. competitors' hybrid assemblies that
  require precise alignment (yield + cost disadvantage)
- **Tier-1 customer design engagements**: Foxconn Interconnect Tech (FOIT),
  Luxshare, Mitsubishi Electric, Celestial AI, Semtech, Sivers Semi, NTT, LITEON
- **Malaysia manufacturing ramp**: Captive production line coming online for
  light source (Q2 2026) and 800G optical engines (Q3 2026)
- **Cash moat**: $430M runway enables ~13 years of development without
  capital raise — removes survival risk

### What POET doesn't have
- **Production revenue yet**: $1.07M in FY2025 is essentially zero
- **Confirmed volume orders**: $5.6M initial production orders disclosed;
  Foxconn / Luxshare still "awaiting feedback" (per CFO comment 4/21)
- **Defensible cost structure**: Fabless with contract-manufactured Malaysia
  capacity = margin depends on volume scaling
- **Incumbent moat**: Competes with silicon photonics from Broadcom, Intel,
  Marvell, Lumentum — all 50–500× larger

### Verdict
**Narrow, pre-validation moat.** IP is real; commercial translation is the
binary question. The company will either become a $5–10B photonics leader
within 3 years or stall out in another 2-year development cycle.

> **"Will the Q2 2026 light-source ramp + Q3 2026 800G engine shipments
> convert to $15M+ in 2026 revenue and $100M+ in 2027 — enough to validate
> the optical interposer thesis before another dilutive raise?"**

All else is noise. The company has been ~2 quarters away from commercial
ramp for ~5 consecutive years. **This time they have actual production orders,
named customers, and operational Malaysian capacity** — which is materially
different from the prior "commercial inflection always next quarter"
pattern. But it's the same binary bet.
