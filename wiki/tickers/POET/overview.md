## Moat

**Narrow and unproven at scale, but architecturally distinctive.**

### What POET has
- **Optical Interposer™ IP portfolio**: Monolithic integration of lasers, modulators,
  detectors on a single substrate — vs. competitors' hybrid assemblies requiring precise
  alignment (yield + cost disadvantage at 800G+)
- **Tier-1 customer design engagements**: Foxconn Interconnect (FOIT), Luxshare,
  Mitsubishi Electric, Celestial AI, Semtech, Sivers Semi, NTT, LITEON
- **Malaysia captive manufacturing**: Production line coming online for light source
  (Q2 2026) and 800G optical engines (Q3 2026)
- **Cash moat**: ~$430M runway = ~12–13 years of development — removes survival risk

### What POET doesn't have
- **Production revenue yet**: $1.07M in FY2025 is essentially zero; all licensing/NRE
- **Confirmed volume orders**: $5.6M initial production orders disclosed; Foxconn /
  Luxshare still "awaiting feedback" (per CFO 4/21)
- **Defensible cost structure**: Fabless model — margins depend on Malaysia volume scale
- **Incumbent moat**: Competes with silicon photonics from Broadcom, Intel, Marvell,
  Lumentum — all 50–500× larger with embedded customer relationships

### Verdict
**Narrow, pre-validation moat.** IP is real; commercial translation is the binary
question. The company will either become a $5–10B photonics leader within 3 years or
stall out in another 2-year development cycle.
