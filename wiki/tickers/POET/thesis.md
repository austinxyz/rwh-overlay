## 1. Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Raison d'être**: Silicon photonics. The AI-datacenter bandwidth explosion requires
optical (not electrical) interconnect at every layer — rack-to-rack, then intra-rack,
eventually chip-to-chip. Current transceiver manufacturing uses discrete parts (laser
+ modulator + detector + lens alignment), which has poor yield, high cost, and high
power draw at 800G+. POET's **Optical Interposer™** monolithically integrates all of
these onto a single die using semiconductor-like wafer processes — promising lower
cost, higher yield, and lower power per bit as speeds scale to 1.6T and 3.2T.

**Strategic position as of 2026-04-26**:
- **FY2025 revenue**: $1.07M (up from $0.04M in FY2024) — effectively still
  pre-commercial; all revenue is licensing / NRE / pilot, no production COGS
- **Two initial production orders** totaling $5.6M+ announced (Q4 2025) — first
  real commercial revenue in company history
- **Q2 2026**: High-volume light source production begins at Malaysia facility
- **Q3 2026**: 800G optical engine production begins
- **FY2026 target**: >30,000 optical engines shipped (400G / 800G / light source)
- **Cash**: ~$430M post-January 2026 raise — ~12–13 year runway at current burn
- **Analyst coverage**: 2 analysts; median target $8.20 (–46% vs current $15.10)

**Pivotal Question**: *Will the Q2 2026 Malaysia light-source ramp + Q3 2026 800G
engine production translate into confirmed volume purchase orders from Foxconn
Interconnect and/or Luxshare — generating $15M+ in FY2026 revenue and $100M+ in
FY2027 — or does this become another "always one quarter away" cycle as it was
from 2021–2024?*

If Foxconn + Luxshare (the two largest datacenter interconnect contract manufacturers
globally) place meaningful POs by H2 2026, the thesis converts from speculative to
credible. If not, the $2.31B market cap on $1M trailing revenue is indefensible.

## 5. Competitive Moat

### The Optical Interposer Bet

The entire POET thesis hinges on one technical proposition: *monolithic photonic
integration is structurally superior to the hybrid-assembly status quo at 800G+*.
If true, POET has a structural yield + cost + power advantage versus:

- **Broadcom** (silicon photonics, dominant at 400G/800G today; $900B mcap)
- **Marvell** (800G optical DSP + integration; $65B mcap)
- **Intel** (silicon photonics, retrenching)
- **Lumentum** (laser / TOSA supplier; ~$8B mcap)
- **Coherent** (COHR, laser / transceiver integration; ~$12B mcap)

### What's Real About the Moat
- **IP portfolio**: Wafer-level monolithic integration patents; proprietary process IP
- **Foxconn/Luxshare partnerships**: These companies are the gatekeepers to hyperscale;
  being their design-in partner is a real positive signal
- **Malaysia captive manufacturing**: Eliminates one supply chain layer; direct cost control
- **Cash moat**: $430M eliminates the survival race against time

### What's Not Real Yet
- No production scale — $1M trailing revenue vs. Broadcom's $2B+ optical business
- No confirmed volume orders from either Foxconn or Luxshare
- Competition is larger, better-funded, and not asleep

### Verdict
**Narrow / emerging / unvalidated.** The technology thesis is defensible on paper.
Commercial moat is 2–3 years from being verifiable. The company will either become a
$5–10B photonics leader within 3 years or stall in another development cycle.
