## 1. Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Raison d'être**: Photonic integration. The AI-datacenter bandwidth explosion
requires optical (not electrical) interconnect at every layer — rack-to-rack,
then intra-rack, eventually chip-to-chip. Current transceiver manufacturing
uses discrete parts (laser + modulator + detector + lens alignment), which
has poor yield, high cost, and high power draw at 800G+. POET's **Optical
Interposer™** monolithically integrates all of these onto a single die using
semiconductor-like wafer processes — promising lower cost, higher yield, and
lower power per bit as speeds scale to 1.6T and 3.2T.

**Strategic position**: Late 2025 into 2026 = transition from "licensing
and design-win story" to **first real commercial production orders**. Key
milestones:
- Q4 2025: Two initial production orders totaling $5.6M+ (first meaningful
  commercial revenue in company history)
- 2026 target: >30,000 optical engines shipped (across 400G / 800G / light
  source categories)
- Q2 2026: High-volume light source production begins (Malaysia)
- Q3 2026: 800G optical engine production begins
- 2027: LITEON high-volume co-developed modules; Foxconn/Luxshare ramp

**Pivotal Question**: *Does the named customer list (Foxconn, Luxshare,
Mitsubishi, Celestial AI, LITEON) translate into confirmed volume POs in
2026 — or does this become another "one more quarter away" cycle like
2021–2024?*

If Foxconn + Luxshare (the two largest data center interconnect manufacturers
globally) place meaningful orders by H2 2026, the thesis converts from
speculative to credible. If not, the $1.95B market cap on $1M trailing
revenue becomes indefensible.

## 5. Competitive Moat

### The Optical Interposer Bet

The entire POET thesis hinges on one technical proposition: *monolithic
photonic integration is structurally superior to the hybrid-assembly status
quo at 800G+*. If true, POET has a structural yield + cost + power advantage
versus:

- **Broadcom** (silicon photonics, dominant at 400G/800G today)
- **Marvell** (800G optical DSP + integration)
- **Intel** (silicon photonics, retrenching)
- **Lumentum** (laser / TOSA supplier)
- **Coherent** (COHR, laser / transceiver integration)
- **II-VI / Finisar** (now part of Coherent)

### The reality check

None of these competitors will stand still. Broadcom alone ships >$2B of
optical products annually, has hyperscale customer embedded relationships,
and is investing aggressively in its own silicon-photonics CPO roadmap. Even
if POET's technology is 20% better, the incumbents' customer depth + process
scale advantage is formidable.

### What's real about the moat
- **IP portfolio** (wafer-level monolithic integration patents)
- **Foxconn/Luxshare partnerships** — these companies are the gatekeepers to
  hyperscale; being their design-in partner is a real positive signal
- **Malaysia captive manufacturing** — eliminates one layer of supply chain risk

### What's unreal
- No scale yet
- No customer concentration data
- Competition is larger, better-funded, and not asleep

### Verdict
**Narrow / emerging / unvalidated.** The technology thesis is defensible on
paper. Commercial moat is 2–3 years from being verifiable.
