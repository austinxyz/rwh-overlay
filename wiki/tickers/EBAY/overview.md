## Business Description

eBay Inc. is a global commerce platform founded in 1995, headquartered in San Jose, California.
It operates a two-sided marketplace connecting buyers and sellers across consumer-to-consumer
(C2C) and business-to-consumer (B2C) models, with zero inventory ownership. eBay's defining
strategic pivot is toward **non-new, in-season goods** — collectibles, refurbished electronics,
automotive parts, luxury goods, and recommerce — categories where it can differentiate from
Amazon's dominance in new goods.

**Core business segments (FY2025)**:
- **Marketplace platform**: ~$11.1B revenue; ~$79B GMV globally; fee-based model on transactions
- **Advertising**: ~$544M revenue; ~2.6% GMV penetration; first-party ad revenue growing fast
- **Payments (managed payments)**: Fully implemented; higher-margin; reduces third-party fee drag
- **Focus Categories** (>25% of GMV): Collectibles, Motors Parts, Luxury Watches, Sneakers,
  Refurbished Electronics — growing 15%+ GMV, outpacing total platform by 11 percentage points
- **Depop** (acquired 2026): $1.2B deal from Etsy; $1B GMV; 7M buyers, 90% under 34;
  high-growth C2C fashion recommerce for Gen Z

FY2025 highlights: GMV ~$79B (+6% FX-neutral); revenue $11.1B (+8%); non-GAAP EPS grew
~13% YoY — second consecutive year of double-digit earnings growth.

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **Is eBay a melting ice cube being structurally disrupted by Amazon and Temu, or a focused
> recommerce platform with durable niche advantages in collectibles + used goods that can
> compound at 10-15% earnings per year via GMV growth + take rate expansion + aggressive buybacks?**

Bull: Focus category strategy (collectibles, motors, luxury) growing 15%+ vs. Amazon's weakness
in used goods; AI-powered listings + authentication create trust moat; aggressive buybacks
(~$2.5B in 2025 alone) compound EPS at 10%+/year even if GMV growth is modest; Depop adds
Gen Z C2C fashion layer.
Bear: Core GMV (~$79B) has barely grown over 5 years; Amazon, Temu, and Shein pressure the
broader marketplace; declining active buyer count is structural; Depop integration risk; $1.2B
for a $1B GMV asset is expensive.

## Moat Rating

- **Rating**: Narrow
- **Sources**:
  - Network effects: 132M active buyers + millions of unique/used/collectible listings — hard to
    replicate the long-tail inventory that makes eBay the default for specific niches
  - Brand: 30-year trust brand for peer-to-peer transactions; authentication programs for watches,
    sneakers reinforce credibility
  - Data: 3 decades of transaction data enables AI-powered pricing, fraud detection, and listing quality
  - Switching costs (seller): Sellers with established ratings/feedback have high platform lock-in
- **Limits on moat**: No inventory or logistics moat; Amazon can and does compete on used goods;
  Mercari, Poshmark, Vinted attack C2C fashion; Bring a Trailer / Copart attack auto; new
  entrants chip at every sub-category
