## Section 1 — Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Question

**Founding Insight**: Pierre Omidyar built eBay in 1995 on a radical premise: that ordinary
people should be able to transact directly with each other, and that an open marketplace
creates more value than a curated store. eBay democratized commerce — enabling individuals
to sell anything to anyone, globally, before Amazon even sold a book.

**The 30-Year Identity Shift**: eBay's original advantage (first-mover in online C2C
marketplaces) has been partially eroded by Amazon's new-goods dominance, Etsy's handmade
niche, and a flood of specialty platforms. The company's survival and renewed growth rests
on a deliberate narrowing of focus: **eBay is repositioning as the world's premier marketplace
for non-new, in-season, enthusiast goods** — collectibles, motors parts, luxury watches,
sneakers, refurbished electronics, and fashion recommerce. Over 40% of GMV in 2025 came
from recommerce. Focus categories grew >15% GMV in Q4 2025, vs. 8% overall.

**AI as the Trust Layer**: eBay's 30 years of transaction data is now being weaponized.
"Magical Listing" (AI auto-populates listing details from a photo) deployed to 10M sellers
with 200M+ listings. Authentication programs (1M+ items inspected quarterly) solve the trust
problem in high-value categories. AI personalizes search and buyer discovery. These reduce
friction on both sides — sellers list faster, buyers trust more.

**The Depop Move**: Acquiring Depop ($1.2B from Etsy, closing H1 2026) is the most
consequential strategic signal in years. Depop = Gen Z C2C fashion recommerce. 7M buyers,
90% under 34, $1B GMV growing 60% in the U.S. eBay's core demographic is aging; Depop
is the Gen Z pipeline.

**The Pivotal Investment Question**:
> Is eBay a melting ice cube being structurally disrupted by Amazon and Temu — or a focused
> recommerce platform with durable niche advantages in collectibles + used goods that can
> compound EPS at 10–15% per year via GMV acceleration + take rate expansion + aggressive buybacks?

The bull: GMV inflecting upward (+6% in FY2025, +8% Q4, guided +10–12% Q1 2026); focus
category moat in niches Amazon can't easily serve; AI trust tools create durable seller supply;
Depop adds Gen Z; aggressive capital return (~$3B/year) compresses share count and amplifies EPS.

The bear: Core GMV of ~$79B has barely recovered to 2021 COVID levels; active buyer count is
structurally declining (~132M vs. 180M+ at peak); Depop at $1.2B for $1B GMV is a rich price;
Amazon's secondhand programs (Amazon Renewed, Amazon Second Chance) are getting better;
Mercari, Poshmark, Vinted, ThredUp attack fashion recommerce; valuation at ~18x forward P/E
is no longer a margin-of-safety price.

**Take Rate Analysis**:
- Net take rate (revenue / GMV) ≈ 14% ($11.1B / $79B) — elevated vs. gross merchandise marketplaces
- Managed Payments eliminates PayPal drag (~3% fee); all net revenue now flows through eBay
- Ad penetration growing (2.6% of GMV) — every 0.1% increase in ad take = ~$79M revenue
- UK private seller re-monetization is incremental take rate expansion with minimal cost

**Capital-Light Model**: No inventory. No warehouses. Sellers ship directly. eBay's capex is
4–5% of revenue (vs. Amazon's ~10%+ ex-AWS). FCF generation is high relative to asset base.

## Section 5 — Competitive Moat

**Rating: Narrow**

**Genuine Moat Sources**:
- **Liquidity network effect**: 132M active buyers × millions of unique listings = the most
  liquid marketplace for non-new goods globally. A vintage Rolex or a rare Pokémon card finds
  better price discovery on eBay than anywhere else.
- **Long-tail inventory**: eBay lists items Amazon won't carry and Walmart can't source. The
  supply side (unique, one-of-a-kind items) is structurally irreplicable in the short term.
- **Trust infrastructure**: 30 years of buyer/seller feedback, authentication programs for
  watches/sneakers/trading cards, eBay Money Back Guarantee. Trust IS the moat in C2C.
- **AI data advantage**: 30 years of transaction data → AI that prices items, predicts fraud,
  auto-populates listings, and personalizes discovery. No new entrant has this dataset.
- **Seller switching costs**: Top-rated sellers with thousands of feedback points and established
  buyer bases are highly reluctant to move to a new platform.

**Limits on Moat**:
- Horizontal marketplace (everything) is harder to defend than vertical (Bring a Trailer for
  cars; Chrono24 for watches; GOAT/StockX for sneakers) — specialists are eroding eBay's edges
- Amazon Renewed, Amazon Second Chance, and Shopify's used goods programs are improving
- Mercari, Poshmark, Vinted attack C2C apparel (largest C2C category)
- Temu and Shein eat the "cheap new goods" segment that eBay B2C sellers relied on
- Active buyer count declining (132M vs. 180M+ peak) — structural signal

**Moat Verdict**: Narrow and narrowing at the edges, but the core collectibles/motors/luxury
niche is genuinely defensible. Focus category strategy is the right response — retreating
to strength rather than fighting Amazon everywhere.

## Section 8 — Strategic Growth Initiatives

1. **Focus Category Deepening**: Collectibles, Motors Parts, Luxury, Sneakers, Refurbished
   Electronics — each receiving tailored authentication, discovery, and pricing tools. Growing
   15%+ vs. 8% overall. Target: 30%+ of GMV by 2027.

2. **Depop Integration**: C2C fashion recommerce; Gen Z pipeline; $1B GMV with 60% U.S. growth.
   AI-powered "personal shopping assistants" planned. Strategic rationale: eBay ages with its
   users; Depop reverses the demographic decay.

3. **AI Magical Listings**: Deployed to 10M sellers, 200M+ listings auto-generated from photos.
   Reduces listing friction → more supply → better buyer selection → higher GMV. Virtuous cycle.

4. **Take Rate Expansion**:
   - Advertising (Promoted Listings): 2.6% GMV penetration; targeting 3%+
   - UK private seller re-monetization (Buyer Protection Fees + Simple Delivery)
   - Managed Payments already fully implemented; no more PayPal revenue drag

5. **eBay Live**: Live shopping / auction integration. Early stage; following Whatnot's playbook
   for collectibles + trading cards. Potential real-time engagement for collectors.

6. **Vehicles**: GMV growth in autos/vehicles emerging as a focus area per Iannone commentary.

7. **Global Recommerce**: Europe recommerce (especially UK/DE C2C) is years behind U.S.;
   UK private seller re-monetization is a 2026–2027 structural tailwind.

8. **AI Personalization**: Buyer-side discovery overhaul — personalized homepage, search results,
   and recommendations to drive repeat purchase frequency.

## Section 10 — Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

**Primary macro drivers for EBAY**:

1. **Recommerce / Secondhand Market Growth** (dominant):
   - Global secondhand market estimated $200B+, growing 15%+ annually (ThredUp data)
   - Environmental and economic tailwinds: Gen Z sustainability mindset + inflation driving
     value-seeking behavior
   - Recommerce is outpacing new goods growth by 3-5x in most categories
   - eBay's 40%+ recommerce GMV share is a structurally growing segment

2. **AI in Marketplace Infrastructure**:
   - AI reduces cost of listing (Magical Listing), fraud detection (trust), and discovery
     (personalization). This is a genuine efficiency gain for a platform business.
   - Early mover with 30 years of data → compounding advantage over new entrants

3. **Consumer Discretionary Sensitivity**:
   - Collectibles, luxury goods, sneakers = discretionary spending → correlated with consumer
     confidence and macro cycles
   - In a recession, some categories (used goods, refurbished electronics) actually benefit
     as consumers trade down; collectibles/luxury could soften

4. **Cross-Border Trade & Tariffs**:
   - eBay facilitates significant cross-border transactions (U.S. buyers from overseas sellers
     and vice versa). Trump-era tariffs on Chinese imports could meaningfully reduce cross-border
     volume in 2026.

5. **Digital Advertising Tailwind**:
   - eBay's ad business (Promoted Listings) benefits from the secular trend of retail media.
     First-party data = high-value ad inventory. Ad revenue growing faster than GMV.

## Section 13 — BAIT Framework

**B — Behavioral: MODERATE**
- Fear: "eBay is a dying platform being killed by Amazon" — a narrative that dominated
  2022–2024 when GMV was flat/declining. This fear is now partially outdated.
- Greed: "eBay is an AI recommerce story trading at a discount to growth" — a newer narrative
  that may be driving the recent +33% run. The stock is no longer pricing in failure.
- Behavioral signal: The re-rating has happened. The easy behavioral alpha (buying the
  "melting ice cube" at trough multiples) has largely been harvested by early movers.

**A — Analytical: MODERATE**
- Consensus likely underestimates: UK private seller take rate expansion (real, multi-year
  tailwind) and advertising GMV penetration growth (2.6% → 3.5%+ is significant)
- Consensus may overestimate: GMV growth sustainability if macro weakens or tariffs bite;
  active buyer count decline is not resolved by focus category GMV growth alone
- Buyback math: ~$2.5B repurchases on ~$47B market cap = ~5% annual float reduction.
  Even 3% GMV growth + 5% float reduction = ~8-10% EPS growth with no margin expansion.
  This is the "quiet compounder" case.

**I — Informational: WEAK**
- Q4 2025 results are widely covered; no informational edge in the earnings data
- Depop acquisition is public and fully debated; no edge there
- The underappreciated data point: UK private seller re-monetization timeline and magnitude
  are not well-modeled by sell-side. This could be a positive surprise in 2026-2027.

**T — Technical: MODERATE**
- Stock +33% in 12 months; at or near all-time high (~$107 on Apr 20, 2026)
- Buyback program ($2.8B authorized) provides a mechanical floor — eBay is its own
  largest buyer at current prices
- No index inclusion catalysts; no spin-offs; no major options events identified

**BAIT Verdict**: B-MODERATE + A-MODERATE + I-WEAK + T-MODERATE = **Single Overlap**
Lower conviction than LLY or other high-BAIT names. The story is good but no longer mispriced.

## Section 15 — Bottom Line

**1-Year Target**: ~$105–$115 (+2–12% upside) — base case execution
**3-Year Target**: ~$130–$160 — if recommerce inflection sustains, Depop integrates, and buybacks compound
**Probability-Weighted EV**: ~$108 (+5% upside at current price)

**Verdict**: **Watch / Small Position** — not a strong buy at $103
- The strategy is correct (recommerce + focus categories + AI trust layer)
- The execution is improving (GMV inflection in FY2025, double-digit EPS growth)
- The buyback program ($2.8B authorized) is a powerful EPS compounding engine
- **But**: The easy money has been made (stock +33%); forward P/E ~18x prices in a lot of good news;
  Depop integration is a near-term margin headwind; active buyer decline is structural

**Best entry**: $85–$95 range on a macro pullback or Depop-related concern.
**Portfolio weight**: 2–4% maximum given the narrow moat and structural headwinds.

**The non-obvious bull case**: eBay's buyback math is the highest-conviction element. If eBay
can maintain ~$2B+ in annual buybacks on a $47B market cap, share count falls 4–5%/year.
Even with 5% GMV growth and flat margins, that's 10–12% annual EPS growth — making the ~18x
forward P/E cheaper every year. This is a "buy and ignore" story for the next 3–5 years if
you can get in at the right price.
