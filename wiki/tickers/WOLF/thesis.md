## 1. Why Does This Company Exist? + Pivotal Investment Question

**Raison d'être**: Silicon carbide (SiC) is a wide-bandgap power
semiconductor material that switches faster, handles higher voltages, and
operates at higher temperatures than silicon. Primary applications: EV
traction inverters, solar inverters, industrial motor drives, and — most
recently — AI datacenter power conversion (NVIDIA 800V HVDC architecture).

Wolfspeed is the **only vertically-integrated SiC manufacturer in the US**,
controlling the entire stack: crystal growth → wafer production → epitaxial
deposition → device fabrication → module assembly. The Durham NC + Mohawk
Valley NY footprint positions WOLF as the **US national champion for SiC**
in an industry dominated by European (STM, Infineon) and Chinese (SICC,
TanKeBlue) competitors.

**Strategic position as of 2026-04-23**:
- **Emerged from Chapter 11 September 29, 2025** after 91-day prepackaged
  case
- Debt reduced from $6.7B to $2B (–70%); interest expense cut 60%
- Existing equity holders received only 3–5% of reorganized company;
  creditors hold ~95%
- Cash ~$1.5B post-emergence (including $700M CHIPS Act Section 48D
  tax refund)
- 200mm Mohawk Valley SiC fab operational; 150mm Durham fab production
  shut down (one month early)
- Q3 FY2026 revenue guide: $140–160M (continued sequential decline)
- Analyst coverage thin: 0 Buy, 1 Hold, 1 Sell; median target $15 (46%
  below current price); Piper Sandler outlier at $20

**Pivotal Question**: Can Mohawk Valley's 200mm fab achieve positive gross
margin by late FY2027 — despite Chinese SiC substrate pricing down 30% YoY
and EV demand remaining weak — so that the restructured entity can actually
generate FCF before its new $2B debt stack begins maturing in 2030?

## 4. Revenue Mix & Business Model

### Segments (pre-bankruptcy nomenclature, subject to restructuring revision)

| Segment | Products | FY2025 Revenue Est | Direction |
|---------|----------|-------------------:|-----------|
| **Power** | SiC MOSFETs, diodes, modules for EV/industrial | ~$500M | Declining (EV weakness) |
| **Materials** | SiC substrates, epi wafers (sold to competitors & internal) | ~$258M | Volatile (pricing pressure) |

**Business model mechanics**:
- Substrate business supplies WOLF's own device business AND competitors
  (customers include STM, Infineon before bankruptcy)
- Device business competes against the same customers in end markets
- **Conflict of interest tension**: Some SiC device customers want to
  source substrates from WOLF's own competitors
- FY2025 Q4 data suggests Power revenue rising as Mohawk Valley ramps;
  Materials revenue flat-to-declining

**Bankruptcy impact on customer relationships**:
- Second-sourcing activity during 91-day case
- Customer inventory builds ahead of Durham closure
- Customer trust damaged; must be rebuilt Q3 FY2026+

## 5. Competitive Moat

### SiC Value Chain Competitive Positioning

| Layer | Global Leader | WOLF Position | Comment |
|-------|--------------|---------------|---------|
| Substrate (SiC wafers) | **WOLF (33.7%)** | **#1** | Only real moat |
| Epitaxial deposition | Fragmented | Competitive | Commodity-ish |
| Device (MOSFETs, diodes) | STM (35%) | **NOT top 3** | onsemi 25%, Infineon 15% |
| Module assembly | Fragmented | Competitive | Low value-add |

### Direct Competitors by Business Line

**SiC Substrate (WOLF's strength)**:
- SICC (China) — fastest-growing, 30% price cuts
- TanKeBlue (China) — aggressive pricing
- II-VI / Coherent (COHR)
- Resonac (Japan, formerly Showa Denko)

**SiC Device (WOLF's weakness)**:
- **STMicroelectronics** — 35% market share, dominant in automotive
- **onsemi** (ON) — 25% share, expanding Czech fab
- **Infineon** — 15%, Europe-focused
- **ROHM** (Japan), Bosch, Navitas (NVTS), Power Integrations

### What's eroding

1. **Chinese substrate capacity expansion** — SICC + TanKeBlue targeting
   50%+ share by 2028; pricing pressure real
2. **EV OEM in-housing** — Tesla already producing own SiC; others evaluating
3. **200mm transition ubiquity** — all major competitors also building 200mm
   fabs; WOLF's first-mover advantage narrowing
4. **Customer bankruptcy overhang** — 3–5 year memory effect on new
   design-win wins

### Verdict
**Narrow moat, actively eroding.** Substrate leadership is real but prices
are falling. Device position is weak. The restructuring gave WOLF balance
sheet survival but not a defensible competitive position. Mohawk Valley
200mm fab is the single best asset — if it can hit yield targets, it could
be the lowest-cost 200mm SiC production in the West.

## 9. Industry-Specific Macro Analysis

### SiC Market Sizing

- Global SiC market 2026: $6.16B projected
- Global SiC market 2036: $11.24B projected (6.2% CAGR)
- SiC module market 2026–2034: $1.26B → $9.82B (29.2% CAGR — faster)

### End-Market Dynamics

**Automotive EV (historical main driver)**:
- US EV demand softer in 2025–2026 due to tariff uncertainty, reduced
  tax credits, high rates
- European EV demand soft; Chinese EV demand strong but serviced
  domestically
- SiC content-per-vehicle rising (~$500–$1,500 per BEV at 800V)
- **Net effect**: Volume-mix-price math is negative for WOLF in 2026

**AI Datacenter (emerging driver)**:
- NVIDIA 800V HVDC architecture (Kyber rack) uses GaN + SiC
- Hyperscale capex $275B+ 2026, with SiC rising share
- But this is 2027–2029 revenue, not 2026

**Industrial / Solar**:
- Stable demand; moderate growth
- Lower ASP than automotive

### Chinese Competitive Dynamic

- SICC, TanKeBlue expanding 200mm substrate capacity aggressively
- Chinese government subsidies + domestic demand = structural cost advantage
- Substrate prices down 30% in 2024; expect another 15–20% in 2026
- **This is existential** for WOLF's substrate business (its strongest asset)

### CHIPS Act Support

- Section 48D tax credit: $700M already received
- Additional ~$300M potential depending on fab commissioning metrics
- Some political risk if CHIPS Act is repealed, but bipartisan support
  has been durable

**Complicated — mid-cycle rebound**. Stock has traveled: $40+ pre-2024 →
$8 low during bankruptcy → $27.75 post-emergence recovery. This is a
**narrative-rotation stock** — went from "dying company" to "phoenix

Chen Yun's WeChat community is enthusiastic on the "SiC revival" theme.
Retail is FOMO-ing into the +245% rally off the low. But the fundamental
picture hasn't changed: –16% gross margin, $2B new debt, 46% analyst

Verdict: **Behavioral signal contrarian-bearish at current price** —
the rebound is running ahead of operational evidence.

### A — Analytical

**Weak-to-negative** with one positive. Forward EV/Revenue (~2.6×) is
in-line with profitable peers, BUT WOLF has negative gross margin and
analyst median implies 46% downside. The only positive: $1.5B cash gives
3+ years to fix operations.

Verdict: **Analytical signal negative** — priced for recovery that hasn't
happened.

### I — Informational

**Moderate — post-restructuring complexity creates edge**. The
bankruptcy/emergence/fresh-start accounting story is complex and
poorly-understood by retail. Investors who carefully parse:
- Pre-vs-post share count
- Debt write-down vs. operating loss
- Mohawk Valley unit economics
- Chinese competitive pricing

...have real informational edge vs. momentum traders. But this edge is
narrow because the most-important data (Q3 FY2026) hasn't been reported.

Verdict: **Informational signal modestly positive**.

### T — Technical

**Extended recovery momentum**. +245% from low, –24% from high. Currently
