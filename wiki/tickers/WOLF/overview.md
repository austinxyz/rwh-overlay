## Business in One Line

Vertically-integrated silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductor manufacturer
— **leader in SiC substrates** (~34% global share), trailing player in SiC
devices — emerging from September 2025 Chapter 11 bankruptcy with balance
sheet de-levered but underlying business still structurally challenged.

## Moat

**Narrow, eroding, with one genuine edge.**

### What WOLF has
- **#1 in SiC substrates globally** — 33.7% market share in wafer-level
  SiC material supply (2024 data)
- **200mm SiC fab at Mohawk Valley, NY** — industry-leading capacity, only
  large-scale 200mm fab operational in US for SiC
- **Vertical integration** — substrate + epi + device + packaging under
  one roof (unique in SiC industry)
- **CHIPS Act position** — $700M tax refund received; eligible for further
  Section 48D credits
- **Post-bankruptcy balance sheet**: debt cut 70%, cash $1.5B, maturity
  2030 (5-yr runway at current burn, though burn must come down)

### What WOLF doesn't have
- **Device market leadership**: STM 35%, onsemi 25%, Infineon 15% lead the
  SiC device market; WOLF is NOT in top 3 for devices
- **Positive unit economics**: FY2025 gross margin was –16% (every dollar
  of revenue cost more than $1 of COGS)
- **EV customer momentum**: Weak EV demand in US/EU has hit SiC harder
  than other segments; Chinese EV preference for local SiC suppliers
- **Price leadership**: Chinese entrants (SICC, TanKeBlue) pushing SiC
  wafer prices down 30% YoY
- **Customer trust**: Bankruptcy caused "second-sourcing activity" —
  customers built buffer stock and qualified backup suppliers

### Verdict
**Narrow moat in substrate + US CHIPS position; weak moat in devices.**
The restructuring fixed the liabilities side of the balance sheet but did
NOT fix the business economics. Returning to positive gross margin requires
pricing recovery (unlikely given Chinese competition) OR Mohawk Valley
yield/cost improvements (possible but takes 18–24 months of execution).

## Pivotal Investment Question

> **"Can Mohawk Valley 200mm fab achieve positive gross margin by late
> FY2027 in a market where SiC ASPs are declining 20–30%/yr due to Chinese
> supply — or will competitive pricing and soft EV demand force a second
> restructuring within 3 years?"**

The balance sheet is fixed. The *business* is not. Every other question
(Yun Chen's bullish framing, EV recovery, data-center SiC demand) is
downstream of this central unit-economics question.

## Why It's In The Wiki

Promoted from `opinions/chen-yun.md`. Yun Chen named WOLF on 2026-03-29
("能源电网变革赛道") and emphasized on 2026-04-16 ("WOLF 今日又会大涨暴涨";
"全球碳化硅半导体材料业几乎……正在美本土大规模扩产"). He frames WOLF as
"the global SiC semiconductor leader" (全球碳化硅半导体龙头).

**Critical framing correction**: Chen's characterization is *partially
incorrect*. WOLF leads in SiC **substrates (wafers/material)** — not SiC
**devices** (where STM, onsemi, Infineon dominate). These are different
parts of the value chain with different economics. Chen's bullish thesis
ignores (a) the September 2025 Chapter 11 emergence, (b) the –16% gross
margin reality, and (c) Chinese substrate competition. This promotion
exists to balance Chen's enthusiasm against the financial reality, not to
endorse it.
